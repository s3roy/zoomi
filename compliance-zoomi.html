Statement of Compliance under the Information Technology
(Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021

Zoomi (hereinafter referred to as "Zoomi") operates as a social media application and is classified as a Social Media Intermediary as defined under the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021 (hereinafter referred to as the “Rules”). In compliance with these Rules, Zoomi has implemented the following policies and procedures.

Zoomi's Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and Community Guidelines (collectively referred to as “Zoomi Policies”) are accessible within the app under the Profile tab. Users will be informed of any changes to these Policies at least once a year. Additionally, Zoomi will notify users annually that non-compliance with the Policies may lead to immediate termination of access, removal of non-compliant information, or both. These notifications will be communicated through push notifications in the Important Notification tab.

To comply with the Rules, Zoomi Policies explicitly prohibit users from publishing content that:

1 Invades another person’s bodily privacy or is harmful or harassing, especially towards children.
2 Is patently false or misleading while appearing as fact.
3 Impersonates or deceives others with the intent to cause harm, wrongful loss, harassment, wrongful gain, or fraud.
4 Threatens the unity, integrity, defense, security, or sovereignty of India.
5 Violates any applicable law.


Zoomi is committed to deploying technology-based measures, including automated tools and other mechanisms, to proactively identify and remove content that falls within the above categories. A strict No Nudity Policy is enforced, and any content or interactions involving nudity will be promptly removed or terminated as necessary.

Any orders from a court of competent jurisdiction or from an appropriate government agency for takedown or related purposes should be directed to the Resident Grievance Officer via email at shivam@zoomiapp.in or through this link.

Zoomi will remove or disable access to any stored, hosted, or published information within 36 hours of receiving a valid court order or notification from the government or its authorized agencies, stating that such information is unlawful or prohibited by law in relation to the sovereignty and integrity of India, security of the state, etc., as outlined in the Rules.

Upon receipt of a written order from a legally authorized government agency that clearly states the purpose, Zoomi will provide any requested information or assistance within its control or possession to the relevant authority within 72 hours.

In compliance with the Rules:

1 User registration information will be retained by Zoomi for 180 days from the date of cancellation or withdrawal of registration.
2 Information that has been removed or access to which has been disabled will be retained for 180 days for investigative purposes, or for such longer periods as required by the court or authorized government agencies.
Zoomi’s grievance handling mechanism is detailed in the section titled Zoomi Process for Grievance Redressal.

Upon receiving a court order or an order from a competent authority as specified under the Rules, Zoomi may facilitate the identification of the first originator of any information deemed necessary under applicable laws and regulations.
